# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially among students and young users who frequently use digital payment apps. This type of fraud usually occurs when a person is tricked into sharing sensitive information or unknowingly approving a fraudulent transaction.

In a typical scenario, the victim receives a call, message, or WhatsApp text from someone pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive. The fraudster may claim that there is an issue with the user’s account, offer cashback, or ask for payment confirmation. They then send a “collect request” on a UPI app and instruct the user to enter their UPI PIN to receive money. However, entering the PIN actually authorizes a payment, resulting in money being deducted from the victim’s account.

In some cases, fake links or QR codes are also shared, which redirect users to fraudulent pages or initiate unauthorized transactions. Students and new users are often targeted because they may not fully understand how UPI systems work.

The consequences include financial loss, panic, and sometimes misuse of personal information. This highlights the importance of digital awareness, careful verification, and responsible use of online payment systems to avoid such frauds.
