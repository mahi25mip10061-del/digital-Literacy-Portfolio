# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist

- Never share your OTP, passwords, or UPI PIN with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank or official organization  
- Do not approve unknown “collect requests” on UPI apps, especially if you are not expecting any payment  
- Always remember that entering your UPI PIN means you are sending money, not receiving it  
- Verify the identity of any caller or message before taking action, especially for financial matters  
- Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments from messages or emails  
- Do not scan QR codes from unknown sources, as they may lead to fraudulent transactions  
- Always double-check the receiver’s details before making any payment  
- Use strong, unique passwords for different accounts and change them regularly  
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security on important accounts  
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for banking or financial transactions  
- Keep your apps and mobile software updated to prevent security vulnerabilities  
- Be cautious of offers like cashback, rewards, or urgent requests that seem too good to be true  

## Reporting Cybercrime

If you become a victim of cybercrime, report it immediately:

- National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in  
- Helpline Number: 1930
